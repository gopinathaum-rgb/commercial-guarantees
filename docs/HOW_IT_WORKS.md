# How It Works

A commercial guarantee progresses through a defined lifecycle.

Every stage records structured information that contributes to the overall execution history.

## 1. Obligation

A business commitment is identified.

Examples include:

- Deliver software
- Complete a construction milestone
- Supply manufactured goods
- Release an advance payment

---

## 2. Assessment

The obligation is evaluated before a guarantee is issued.

Assessment may consider:

- contractual requirements
- project information
- supporting documentation
- operational risk

---

## 3. Guarantee

A guarantee is created.

It defines:

- the obligation
- participating parties
- guarantee conditions
- lifecycle state

---

## 4. Evidence

Evidence is attached as execution progresses.

Examples:

- signed contracts
- invoices
- milestone reports
- inspection reports
- delivery confirmations

Evidence is accumulated rather than reconstructed later.

---

## 5. Monitoring

The guarantee remains active while obligations are being performed.

Events are observed throughout execution.

---

## 6. Claim

If guarantee conditions are believed to have been met, a claim may be submitted.

Claims become part of the guarantee history.

---

## 7. Settlement

Approved claims produce settlement records.

Settlement records document the outcome without replacing the original evidence.

---

## 8. Verification

An independent reviewer should be able to reconstruct the guarantee lifecycle from recorded facts.

Verification asks:

- Was the guarantee issued?
- What evidence exists?
- What events occurred?
- Was a claim submitted?
- How was it settled?

The objective is transparent reconstruction rather than manual interpretation.
