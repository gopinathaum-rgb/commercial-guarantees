# Architecture

Commercial Guarantees is organized as a lifecycle rather than a collection of isolated documents.

Each guarantee progresses through a sequence of well-defined stages.

## Core Concepts

### Obligation

A business commitment that may be guaranteed.

Examples include:

- software delivery
- milestone completion
- advance payment
- manufacturing supply

---

### Assessment

Evaluates whether a guarantee should be issued.

Assessment considers available information before commitment.

---

### Guarantee

Represents an issued commercial guarantee.

The guarantee becomes the central object that links the remaining lifecycle.

---

### Evidence

Evidence records observable facts throughout execution.

Examples include:

- contracts
- reports
- invoices
- approvals
- inspections

---

### Monitoring

Tracks guarantee progress while obligations are being performed.

---

### Claim

Records a request to enforce guarantee conditions.

Claims do not determine outcomes by themselves.

---

### Settlement

Documents the outcome of an approved claim.

Settlement becomes part of the guarantee history.

---

### Verification

Verification reconstructs the guarantee lifecycle using recorded facts.

The objective is independent review rather than manual reconstruction.
